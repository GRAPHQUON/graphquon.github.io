GraphQUON 2024 - Code of Conduct

Welcome

All are welcome at the event. We expect all community members to act in accordance with this code of conduct, which is anchored in the belief that the Computer Graphics community thrives on the diversity of its members. The free exchange of ideas is essential to a successful conference and research community. Our intention is to create a space where every participant can express themselves without fear of judgment or harassment.

Should you feel threatened or unsafe, please reach out to the organizer. We will address your concerns promptly.

Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to conduct themselves in accordance with socially expected professional behavior, including but not limited to:

  • Displaying their full name as registered on platforms (e.g., Discord, Zoom).
  • Not publicly sharing the Zoom meeting link.
  • Treating everyone with respect and consideration.
  • Being mindful and respectful of your fellow participants.
  • Being considerate of differing views and opinions.
  • Providing respectful and constructive critique of ideas.
  • Respecting participants' wishes to not be photographed or screen-captured*.
  • Respecting other participants’ right to engage or disengage in conversation.
  • Respecting other participants’ gender pronouns.

*Due to the virtual nature of the event, many interactions will be automatically recorded, and some recordings may be persistent. Individual-level opt-out is not feasible.

Unacceptable Behavior

We are committed to providing a harassment-free experience for all participants. Harassment in any form will not be tolerated. Participants violating these rules may be removed from the event. Harassment includes but is not limited to:

  • Comments targeting others based on characteristics such as gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, age, ability, appearance, or religion.
  • Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following.
  • Unwelcome personal attention.
  • Persistent unwanted attempts to contact another participant.
  • Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors.

Moderation Guidelines

We encourage participants to work out disagreements or misunderstandings amongst themselves first. If this is not effective, please inform an event organizer.

Comments, posts, or discussions deemed as harassment will be removed by the organizational team. We trust participants to conduct themselves professionally, but the following steps will be taken if necessary:

  • Level 1: First-time offending posts or comments will be removed or not approved if applicable.
  • Level 2: Continued violations will result in a private conversation with a team member to explain the infraction and consequences.
  • Level 3: Further violations will result in the participant being banned from the event spaces (e.g., Zoom, Discord). Their identity and infractions will be reported to the organizer.

For egregious first-time offenses, the organizing team reserves the right to ban a participant immediately.
